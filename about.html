The central point of the project, the agent-based model, is implemented and currently under validation. Given that businesses and commercial activities have already been normalised, our first planned activity is to characterise the impact of reopening schools, from nurseries to universities, based on detailed datasets collected from these institutions. The model successfully depicts the expected behaviour of the pandemics and clearly predicts the impact of schools in the city of Natal, though we still need careful review from peers.

We correlate small demographic regions within the city of Natal with the outcomes of the pandemics, namely the number of cases and the number of deaths. For that, we did a comprehensive search for public and private datasets, which had to be pre-processed due to differences in formatting and occasional inconsistencies. Our analyses confirmed that wage and human development index are closely related to the severity of the pandemics, even in regions where the average income is high, regardless of geographical position. This information is crucial for the health authority to plan where and how often COVID19 tests should be done, and which hospitals could be more vulnerable in terms of ICU beds.
